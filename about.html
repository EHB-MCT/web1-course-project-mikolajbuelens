So, who am I?

Well as you might have guessed I'm Mikolaj (yup, a Polish name), as I'm making this website I'm 22 years old and in my first year of Multimedia & creative technology. I have a passion for computers, programming, graphic design, 3D and technology in general. My interest in these topics began at a young age when I first started playing video games around the year 2006. Besides the fun and relaxation I got out of them I also found something deeper, an appreciation for both the creative and technical aspects of said video games. Being born right in time to see the rapid progress in graphics, size and details that were achieved was fascinating. From making grass to entire forests, character, interfaces,... the list goes on and on. And to realize that all these concepts were programmed and designed by people that worked tirelessly on them is nothing short of amazing to me. You can of course extend this to a bunch of other media like movies, where CGI can make or break a scene. Whether I'll be evolving towards the gaming industry in terms of future work is an uncertainty, as it's not an easy sector to be a part of, combine that with the fact that Belgium isn't exactly a market leader in gaming and you got yourself a guy that is making a website right now.

My programming journey

My struggle with learning to program has led me through a path of learning. I'm someone that, if committed to something (in this case learning HTML, CSS, Javascript) wants to be good at it from the get-go but as anyone who has some experience will likely tell you, it's not something you can just learn on a couple of days. Just like you would't be able to learn to speak fluent Chinese in a short amount of time. Now combine that with me being both a perfectionist and not having the patience when a computer doesn't do what I want it to and you might understand why my early days of programming weren't the most enjoyable. However, as time went by and I started to practice more and more (kinda had to if I wanted to pass my year) and reading a lot (documentation and tips from people more experienced than me) I found that instead of being a chore that I had to do, programming became more and more of a fun hobby to the point that I wanted to do it just for the fun of it. And well here I am making this website not because I feel an obligation but because I like doing it.
